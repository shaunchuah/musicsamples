Quick Reference

PAXgene Blood ccfDNA Tube

Tube website: www.preanalytix.com

Important considerations: Blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes cannot be stored for more than 7 days without compromising cfDNA and must be spun down to plasma (which can be frozen) within this time.

Protocol:

  1. Collect blood in accordance to the guidelines (www.preanalytix.com) outlined by PreAnalytiX for PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes
  2. After collection, gently invert PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes 8 times.
  3. Store PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes upright at room temperature (15oC to 27oC) for up to 7 days. Do not allow blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes to drop below 2oC.
  4. For on-site preparation of plasma from PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes, please go straight to step 6.
  5. Transport: Blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes fall under regulation UN3373, Biological Substance, Category B and packaging must be fully compliant with IATA DGR Packaging Instruction 650 requiring labelling instructions and a triple packaging concept with primary receptacle, secondary absorbent package and rigid outer packaging. PAXgene Blood ccfDNA Tubes (CE-IVD) act as a primary receptacle. Blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes must be transported at room temperature (2oC and 30oC) and if not processing on site (step 6), must be transported to QMRI/University of Edinburgh within 7 days of taking blood.
  6. To obtain cell-free plasma, centrifuge blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes at 1000 x g for 10mins at 4oC. Remove resulting plasma from PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tube avoiding buffy coat (as a guide take upper 2/5th of initial blood volume in plasma, this should be around 4 ml plasma from 10 ml whole blood) and transfer into a tube with a layer of 1 ml 90% Percoll. Centrifuge plasma at 16000 x g for 10 mins at 4oC allowing platelets to be cushioned into the Percoll without rupturing.
