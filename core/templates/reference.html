PAXgene Blood ccfDNA Tube

Tube website: www.preanalytix.com

Important considerations: Blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes cannot be stored for more than 7 days without compromising cfDNA and must be spun down to plasma (which can be frozen) within this time.

Protocol:

  1. Collect blood in accordance to the guidelines (www.preanalytix.com) outlined by PreAnalytiX for PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes
  2. After collection, gently invert PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes 8 times.
  3. Store PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes upright at room temperature (15oC to 27oC) for up to 7 days. Do not allow blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes to drop below 2oC.
  4. For on-site preparation of plasma from PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes, please go straight to step 6.
  5. Transport: Blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes fall under regulation UN3373, Biological Substance, Category B and packaging must be fully compliant with IATA DGR Packaging Instruction 650 requiring labelling instructions and a triple packaging concept with primary receptacle, secondary absorbent package and rigid outer packaging. PAXgene Blood ccfDNA Tubes (CE-IVD) act as a primary receptacle. Blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes must be transported at room temperature (2oC and 30oC) and if not processing on site (step 6), must be transported to QMRI/University of Edinburgh within 7 days of taking blood.
  6. To obtain cell-free plasma, centrifuge blood filled PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes at 1000 x g for 10mins at 4oC. Remove resulting plasma from PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tube avoiding buffy coat (as a guide take upper 2/5th of initial blood volume in plasma, this should be around 4 ml plasma from 10 ml whole blood) and transfer into a tube with a layer of 1 ml 90% Percoll. Centrifuge plasma at 16000 x g for 10 mins at 4oC allowing platelets to be cushioned into the Percoll without rupturing.

MUSIC Blood SOPs Glasgow/Dundee

PAXgene Blood ccfDNA tubes: 1 per patient – 9ml blood

  1. Collect blood according to PAXgene ccfDNA guidance and gently invert 8 times.
  2. Label tubes with study ID and sampling date and time. Tubes can be kept at room temperature (2-27C) for up to 7 days
  3. Within 7 days of collection, centrifuge PAXgene ccfDNA tubes at 1600 x g for 10 mins at room temperature
  4. Assess haemolysis using the reference haemolysis chart (in the add new sample form)
  5. Aliquot plasma into 1ml aliquots and centrifuge at 16000 x g for 10mins at room temperature
  6. Place resulting cell free plasma in 1.5-2ml screw-top tubes and register onto G-Trac. Please fill study ID, date/time of original specimen blood collection, date/time of centrifugation (processing datetime) and haemolysis score (1-3).
  7. Freeze cell free plasma at -80C.

PAXgene Blood RNA tubes: 1 per patient – 2.5ml blood

  1. Collect blood according to PAXgene RNA guidance, gently invert 8 times.
  2. Label tubes with study ID and sampling date and time.
  3. Label each tube with QR code and register sample onto G-Trac.
  4. After 2 hours but within 24 hours freeze at -20 before subsequent transfer after 24 hours to -80C for fortnightly/monthly collection.

EDTA tubes: 2 per patient (if possible)

  1. Collect blood and gently invert 8 times.
  2. Within 2 hours, centrifuge at 1000 x g for 10mins at 4C, transfer plasma to sterile tube and centrifuge again at 3000 x g for 10mins at 4C.
  3. Divide into 1 ml aliquots in screw-top tubes.
  4. Label each child aliquot with QR code and register sample onto G-Trac.
  5. Immediately freeze at -80oC for fortnightly/monthly collection.

Notes:

  1. Please read guidance before taking blood with PAXgene ccfDNA tubes.
  2. Blood filled PAXgene ccfDNA tubes must not fall below 2oC.
  3. Blood filled PAXgene ccfDNA tubes must be spun down to plasma within 7 days of blood collection.
  4. Gentle acceleration/deceleration – suggestion set to 5/5 respectively.
  5. Score haemolysis by assessing colour of plasma using this chart, 1 = minimal, 3 = 50 mg/dL. Plasma with a score of 4 or higher (≤ 100 mg/dL) is not to be used for MUSIC.
  6. Blood filled PAXgene RNA tubes are only stable for 3 days at room temperature. Please see guidance for optimum processing and freezing. Do not freeze in Styrofoam tray, please use open, plastic tray.
  7. Screw-top tubes are required for storage in QMRI, Edinburgh, to avoid freeze/thaw cycles, please aliquot into screw-top tubes in the first instance.
